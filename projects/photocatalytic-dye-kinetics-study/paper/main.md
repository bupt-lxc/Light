# Response-Surface Constrained Kinetic Analysis of Visible-Light Dye Photodegradation on Nitrogen-Modified TiO2

**Synthetic English journal-style demonstration paper for Light Skills**

The canonical paper source is [`main.tex`](main.tex). `main.pdf` is compiled from LaTeX by `scripts/build_paper.py`.

## Abstract

This synthetic study demonstrates a reproducible environmental-chemistry workflow for visible-light dye photodegradation. A deterministic generator produced 4968 UV-vis time records across 183 experimental conditions covering catalyst formulation, catalyst loading, pH, irradiance, and radical-scavenger perturbations. Pseudo-first-order fits recovered an optimum condition using N-TiO2/H2O2 at 0.50 g L^-1, pH 7.0, and 105 mW cm^-2, with kobs = 0.0296 min^-1 and an apparent half-life of 23.4 min.

## Honesty boundary

All concentration traces are synthetic and generated by `scripts/generate_data.py`. The study demonstrates a science-style workflow package; it does not claim a real photocatalyst discovery.
